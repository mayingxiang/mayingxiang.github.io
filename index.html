Yingxiang (Andy) Ma马英翔

Ph.D. Candidate, Vanderbilt University  ·  Machine Learning for Protein Science

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Walker Lab at Vanderbilt University, advised by Dr. Allison Walker, where I use machine learning to study proteins. I also hold an M.S. in Data Science from Vanderbilt and a B.S. in Statistics from Capital Normal University.

I am drawn to problems where rigorous science meets practical engineering, and I enjoy translating complex technical ideas for different audiences. Feel free to reach out to talk about protein machine learning, data science, or turning research into real-world tools.

Email/ GitHub/ LinkedIn/ Google Scholar

Research

I am broadly interested in applying machine learning to problems in protein biology — how a protein’s sequence and structure give rise to its function. My current work sits at the interface of modern deep learning and the life sciences.
